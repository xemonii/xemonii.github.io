[ ANKLYOSAURUS ]


> The Armored Dinosaur

General Discovery Description Paleobiology

General

The Ankylosaurus, was an armored dinosaur roaming the shrubbery uplands of North America around the Late Cretacous Period, dating back 70 to 66 million years ago. It is monotypic, only containing A. Magniventris, a name that means "fused lizard" and "great belly".

The genus Ankylosaurus belongs to the infraorder Ankylosauria, which existed throughout the Cretaceous Period and as far back as 145.5 million years ago. The Ankylosaurus magniventris was present in the Late Cretaceous Period and the Cretaceous-Paleogene extinction event. Based on the location in which archeologists find the fossils, the great-bellied lizard likely occupied uplands, away from coastlines. Among their habitat, Ankylosaurus would have encountered the Triceratops, Torosaurus, Edmontosaurus, Thescelosaurus, and Pachycephalosaurus. Additonally, there were theropods such as Ornithomimus and Tyrannosaurus.

Discovery

The first Ankylosaurus fossil was originally found by Peter Kaisen. However, paleontologist Barnum Brown led the expedition back in 1906 funded by the American Museum of Natural History, and scientifically described the fossil. It took Brown until 1908 to map out the Ankylaurus's anatomy and hypothesize it's behaviour, habitat, and diet. Brown also gave the organism it's genus name, Ankylaurus. Ankylosaurus is believed to be the largest of ankylosaurid, the largest known skull measures 64.5 centimeters (25 inches) long and 74.5 centimeters (29 inches) wide.

Description

Ankylosauruses were four-legged armored herbivores. They had flat, squarish heads that were wider than their lenghth. Their bodies were short and low to the ground; the largest Ankylosauruswas likely 10 meters long and weighed up to four tons, their legs were thick and massive to support their weight. Its armor has bone plates called osteoderms. The plates vary in shape and size and fit together, covering the organism's back. The plates get smaller toward and onto the tail, and its tail is tipped with a bone club. Paleontologists first speculated the various uses of the tail club, such as mating display, but it's now assumed to have been used as defense against carnivorous dinosaurs.

Paleobiology

Ankylosaurus was herbivorous, it's wide muzzle was adapted for non-selective low-browse cropping; with leaf-shaped teeth suitable for cropping vegetation, observation of the Ankylosaurus skull concluded that their jaws were likely relatively weak, further suggesting the dinosaur consumed soft vegetation, such as various leaves, ferns, shrubs, and pulpy fruits but avoided woody and fibrous plants. The armored dinosaur's diet was likely as high as 60 kilograms of plants per day.
